  # July 2024
  ## Logistics
  - Stayed at an AirBnb in Carolina/neighborhood area of Isla Verde
  - Flew in to San Juan, no car rental

  ## Itinerary

  ### 7/3
  - Flew in (ended up getting there very late due to delays)
  - Doordashed dinner from Panaderia Mallorca de la 65
  - La Placita

  ### 7/4
  - El Yunque National Forest tour
  - Picked up lunch from Cafeteria Restaurant in Norte Shopping Center (pickup/dropoff point of the tour). Tour usually takes you on a food stop on the way back, but they did not this day due to the holiday
  - Nap, attempt to blow dry and clean mud off my sneakers
  - Sunset dinner at Kumo Rooftop
  - Club Brava

  ### 7/5
  - Lunch at Panaderia Espana
  - Isla Verde Beach
  - Dinner at Bebo's Cafe
  - La Placita
  - Quick bite from Los Chamos Arepas in Carolina

  ### 7/6
  - Old San Juan
    - Lunch at Don Tako
    - Shopping, sightseeing, etc. 
    - Dinner at Birra y Empanadas
    - Sweet treat from Drunken Cookies
  - Flew out

  # March 2025
  ## Logistics
  - Stayed at an AirBnb in Condado
  - Flew into San Juan, no car rental

  ## Itinerary

  ### 3/11
  - Early morning flight (arrived around 3pm)
  - Quick bite at Takeria
  - Condado Beach, explored Condado/Ashford Ave, got some groceries
  - Sandwiches from Stop & Go
  - La Placita

  ### 3/12
  - El Yunque National Forest tour
  - Stop in Luquillo Beach area for food. Ate at Edelweiss Bar & Grill
  - Condado Beach
  - Dinner at Los Chamos Arepas on Ashford Ave.
  - Walked around Ashford Ave., got froyo
  
  ### 3/13
  - Slow morning
  - Old San Juan
    - Late lunch at Cafe El Bosque
    - Sightseeing & souvenir shopping
  - Dinner at Kumo Rooftop
  - Pictures on Isla Verde Beach

  ### 3/14
  - Breakfast at Chocobar Cortes
  - Chilled inside due to bad weather
  - Sunset at Condado Beach
  - Picked up dinner from Los Chamos Arepas (I think)
  - La Placita

  ### 3/15
  - Suuuuper early morning flight out

  ## Must Dos/Notes
  - Los Chamos Arepas in Carolina is the better location and is so so good
  - Kumo Rooftop food and views are 10/10
  - July is definitely part of the rainy season but the ocean is still very warm, just don't expect to get a tan like you would in March
  - I would do the <a href="https://www.airbnb.com/experiences/3433406?s=67&unique_share_id=d7bf4f37-4d19-4016-84e5-d93e4d00b33e" target="_blank">El Yunque Tour</a> a million times again
